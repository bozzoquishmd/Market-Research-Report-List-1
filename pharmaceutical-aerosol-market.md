<p><p>The pharmaceutical aerosol market primarily features two key types: Metered Dose Inhalers (MDIs) and Dry Powder Inhalers (DPIs). MDIs deliver a specific dose of medication in aerosol form through a propellant, allowing for precise dosing and portability. In contrast, DPIs dispense medication in powdered form, which patients inhale deep into the lungs, relying on their breathing effort for activation. Both inhaler types serve distinct therapeutic applications, particularly in respiratory diseases like asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).</p></p>
