<p><p>Buffered Oxide Etchants (BOE) are chemical solutions used primarily in semiconductor manufacturing for etching silicon dioxide layers while protecting underlying materials. The two main types of BOE are BOE 6:1 and BOE 7:1, referring to the ratio of ammonium fluoride to hydrofluoric acid. BOE 6:1 is commonly employed for more aggressive etching, while BOE 7:1 offers a milder option, allowing for precise control in delicate processes. Both variants are essential in microfabrication for creating intricate electronic components.</p></p>
