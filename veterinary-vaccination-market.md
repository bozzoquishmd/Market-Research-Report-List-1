<p><p>The veterinary vaccination market comprises several types, primarily including live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and others. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, stimulating robust immune responses while minimizing disease risk. Inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens to induce immunity without causing infection, making them safer but often requiring multiple doses. Other market types may include subunit, toxoid, and DNA vaccines, each designed for specific diseases and animals, offering varied efficacy and safety profiles in veterinary medicine.</p></p>
