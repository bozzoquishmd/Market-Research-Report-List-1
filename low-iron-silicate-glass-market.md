<p><p>The low iron silicate glass market primarily comprises two types: rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass, produced by passing molten glass through rollers, offers enhanced strength and aesthetics, making it suitable for architectural applications. Float glass, created by floating molten glass on molten tin, provides a uniform thickness and high optical clarity, ideal for windows and facades. Both types are valued for their minimal iron content, resulting in improved transparency and light transmission, catering to various industries including construction and automotive.</p></p>
