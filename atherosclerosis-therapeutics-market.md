<p><p>The Atherosclerosis therapeutics market comprises two primary types: small molecules and biologics. Small molecules, typically oral medications, primarily target lipid levels and inflammation to reduce plaque formation in arterial walls. Examples include statins and PCSK9 inhibitors. Biologics, on the other hand, are larger, more complex therapeutic agents, often derived from living organisms. They may include monoclonal antibodies and gene therapies that specifically target underlying mechanisms of atherosclerosis, offering a more individualized treatment approach. Together, they provide diverse options for managing cardiovascular health.</p></p>
