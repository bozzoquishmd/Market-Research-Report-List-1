<p><ul><li>Structure Based Drug</li><li>Ligand Based Drug</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Computer-Aided Drug Design (CADD) encompasses two primary market types: structure-based drug design (SBDD) and ligand-based drug design (LBDD). SBDD focuses on using the three-dimensional structures of biological targets, such as proteins, to design molecules that fit precisely, enhancing efficacy and reducing side effects. Conversely, LBDD utilizes known ligand information to develop new compounds by analyzing the binding affinity and interactions with the target. Both approaches leverage computational tools to accelerate drug discovery and improve therapeutic outcomes.</p></p>
